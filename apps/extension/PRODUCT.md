# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary: **researchers** — academics, analysts, journalists, and serious knowledge workers who do deep source-based inquiry and need to trace every claim back to its origin. Secondary: students doing literature reviews and independent builders who want a local-first AI knowledge base. They use the extension while actively browsing — tab open, reading in progress — so the interface must be fast to invoke, dense with information, and invisible until needed.

## Product Purpose

Magpie lives in your browser's side panel. It gleefully captures web pages, PDFs, academic papers, and YouTube transcripts into your personal Lore, then answers questions across that knowledge with citations linking to the exact paragraph the answer came from. The deep research agent runs multi-agent web + academic + news searches autonomously, indexes every source as a real library document, and synthesizes a report where every claim is traceable. Success looks like: the user never has to wonder where a fact came from.

## Positioning

Every AI answer cites the exact paragraph it came from. Every source lives in your Lore.

## Brand Personality

**Precise instrument, not a toy.** The extension signals seriousness through compression, structure, and traceability — never through decoration. Voice is terse, informative, action-oriented. Three-word personality: **rigorous, legible, commanding.** Emotional goal: the user should feel in control of their research, not dependent on a black box. The interface earns trust by showing its work — citation chips, source counts, research logs — rather than hiding complexity behind a friendly chat bubble.

## Anti-references

- **Academic / institutional.** Stiff, dense serif typography, muted grey everything, the visual language of a university library catalogue. This is serious without being dull; rigour should feel alive.
- **Consumer AI chat** (ChatGPT, Claude web UI). Alternating bubble chat, soft rounded surfaces, minimal chrome. Every answer cites a source; the interface should reflect that density, not flatten it.
- **Generic SaaS** (purple-blue gradients, hero metrics, card grids). No decorative data visualisations, no identical card layouts, no gradient text.
- **Note-taking productivity tools** (Notion aesthetic). Rounded corners, soft shadows, lots of white space, emoji-led hierarchy. Too casual for a research instrument.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the work.** Citation chips, source counts, agent logs, and phase labels are first-class UI — not collapsible noise. The user's confidence scales with their ability to trace every claim.
2. **Compression is respect.** The side panel is narrow and the user is busy. Pack meaning into fewer pixels; use micro-type, inline structure, and progressive disclosure rather than spreading information thin.
3. **Precision over warmth, legibility over minimalism.** The design is not warm or friendly; it is clear, exact, and trustworthy. Contrast must be unambiguous. Every interactive element must announce itself.
4. **The library is the product.** Captured pages and papers are not ephemeral context — they are the user's research asset. The design should reinforce ownership: every source is visible, navigable, and citable.
5. **One aesthetic decision, held consistently.** The terminal/command-post language (monospace, uppercase labels, hard borders, offset shadows) is a deliberate voice — not a default. Every new surface should speak the same dialect.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 against background in both light and forced-dark modes. All interactive elements keyboard-accessible. Reduced-motion support on all transitions and research-log animations.
