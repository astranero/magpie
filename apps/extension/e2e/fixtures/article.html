Photosynthesis Basics

Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants convert light energy into chemical energy. Chlorophyll in the chloroplasts absorbs sunlight, and this drives the synthesis of glucose from carbon dioxide and water.

The light-dependent reactions occur in the thylakoid membranes, where water is split and oxygen is released as a byproduct. The resulting ATP and NADPH power the Calvin cycle.

In the Calvin cycle, carbon dioxide is fixed into an organic molecule by the enzyme RuBisCO. A series of reduction reactions then produces glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate, which the plant uses to build glucose and other carbohydrates.
